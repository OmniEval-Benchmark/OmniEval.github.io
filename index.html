OmniEval: A Benchmark for Evaluating Omni-modal Models with Visual, Auditory, and Textual Inputs

Yiman Zhang, Ziheng Luo, Qiangyu Yan, Wei He, Borui Jiang, Xinghao Chen, Kai Han
The authors are with the Center for Research on Foundation Models (CRFM), Shanghai AI Laboratory. Kai Han is also with The University of Hong Kong.
Paper arXiv Code Dataset

摘要 (Abstract)

Recent advancements in Large Language Models (LLMs) have led to the development of multi-modal models capable of processing both visual and textual information. However, the real world is omni-modal, requiring the simultaneous comprehension of visual, auditory, and textual inputs. Existing benchmarks are insufficient for evaluating such complex capabilities. To address this gap, we introduce OmniEval, a novel benchmark designed to evaluate omni-modal models. OmniEval comprises 2,000 question-answer pairs that demand the joint understanding of all three modalities. Our questions are categorized into two types: perception and reasoning, and span across five real-world scenarios: daily life, driving, sports, surveillance, and entertainment. We evaluate several state-of-the-art multi-modal models on OmniEval and find that their performance is significantly lacking, with the best model achieving an accuracy of only 45.8%. This highlights the substantial challenges posed by omni-modal understanding and the need for further research in this area. We will release the OmniEval benchmark to facilitate future development.
